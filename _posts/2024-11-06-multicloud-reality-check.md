We work across AWS, Azure, and GCP daily, so we have no platform allegiance in this debate. With that disclosure: most multi-cloud strategies we review are not strategies. They are one primary cloud plus an acquisition that came with another, or a single team's preference that calcified into architecture.

Genuine reasons to run multiple clouds exist: regulatory mandates, a specific managed service worth the overhead, customer data residency requirements. Portability as an abstract virtue is not on that list. Building to the lowest common denominator forfeits the managed services that justify cloud pricing in the first place.

Our guidance: pick a primary, go deep, and document the specific conditions under which you would add a second. Optionality you never exercise is just cost.
