Three years ago, cloud cost work meant a quarterly spreadsheet exercise and a round of finger-pointing. Watching the discipline mature has been one of the quieter satisfactions of our practice.

The clients doing FinOps well in 2025 share a shape: unit economics defined per product (cost per order, per active user, per pipeline run), full allocation through enforced tagging, engineers seeing the cost of their own services weekly, and commitments managed centrally with actual forecasting. Cost reviews happen in engineering standups, not finance meetings.

The newer wrinkle is AI workloads, where GPU spend can swamp everything else on the bill. The same principles apply, but the feedback loop has to be faster. We will have more to say on that this year.
