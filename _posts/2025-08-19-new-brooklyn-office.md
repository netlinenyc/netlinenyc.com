A short company note. This month we opened a second workspace in Downtown Brooklyn, complementing our original Manhattan office near Bryant Park.

The reasoning is practical. Our team has grown past twenty people, a meaningful share of whom live in Brooklyn, and several of our client engagements are now with companies based there. The new space gives us room for the workshop-style sessions we run during discovery phases, which had outgrown our Manhattan conference room.

Both offices operate identically: same security practices, same equipment standards, no client data on local machines in either location. Client meetings can be booked at whichever location is convenient.

We remain, as the name suggests, a New York firm. Now slightly more of one.
