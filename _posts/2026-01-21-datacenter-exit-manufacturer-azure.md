We recently completed an eleven-month program moving a specialty manufacturer's complete IT estate to Azure, driven by a datacenter lease expiry that set an immovable deadline.

The estate was typical of the category: an ERP system with deep customizations, file servers holding two decades of CAD data, shop-floor integrations that could not tolerate latency surprises, and backup infrastructure of uncertain reliability. We sequenced the program around the ERP, rehearsed its cutover three times in an isolated environment, and used Azure ExpressRoute to keep shop-floor traffic within tested latency bounds.

The final rack was powered down four weeks before lease end. Deadline-driven migrations reward pessimistic scheduling; the buffer absorbed two vendor delays that would otherwise have been crises.
