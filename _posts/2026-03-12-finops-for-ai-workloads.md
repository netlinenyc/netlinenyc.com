A year ago we predicted AI workloads would stress-test FinOps practices. That has held up. The patterns we now see in client environments: inference costs that scale with usage in ways product teams did not model, fine-tuning jobs left running over weekends, and GPU capacity reserved out of scarcity fear and then underused.

The discipline transfers, but the cadence does not. A monthly cost review is fine for a steady web estate; an AI feature can burn a quarter's budget in a week. Our current baseline for AI-heavy clients: cost per request and per training run defined before launch, spend visible daily, hard budget alerts wired to paging, and a standing question in every review of whether a smaller model would do.
