The loudest infrastructure debate of 2024 is whether companies should leave the cloud. As usual, the interesting answer is unfashionably specific.

Repatriation math works for workloads that are large, steady, and predictable: a constant compute footprint, heavy storage with high egress, little use of managed services. If that describes your estate and you have the operational bench to run hardware, owning servers can genuinely cost less.

It does not describe most of our clients. Variable load, lean platform teams, and heavy reliance on managed databases and queues all tilt the math back toward cloud. Our position: run the numbers per workload, not per ideology. We have helped clients move workloads in both directions this year, and both moves were correct.
