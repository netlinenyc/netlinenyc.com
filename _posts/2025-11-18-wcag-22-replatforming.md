title: "WCAG 2.2 as a forcing function for UI cleanup"
date: 2025-11-18
category: App Design
excerpt: "Teams treating WCAG 2.2 alignment as pure compliance are missing a cheap opportunity to fix long-standing UI debt."
---

More of our clients are being asked, by enterprise customers and by counsel, to demonstrate WCAG 2.2 alignment. The reflexive response is to treat it as a compliance tax. We have been arguing for a different frame.

The newer success criteria, around focus visibility, target sizes, and reducing redundant data entry, overlap almost entirely with ordinary usability debt. Every remediation we have run this year ended up fixing problems users had been quietly tolerating: tap targets too small on mobile, focus indicators removed for aesthetics years ago, forms that ask for the same information twice.

Our suggestion: fund the work once, as a usability project with an accessibility acceptance bar, rather than twice as separate compliance and UX efforts.
