We recently completed a phased Azure migration for a regional insurance carrier exiting two leased datacenters. The engagement covered roughly 140 workloads: policy administration, document management, and a long tail of departmental applications.

Our approach was deliberately unexciting. Phase one rehosted low-risk workloads to validate networking, identity, and backup patterns. Phase two moved the regulated core under a defined change-control window. Phase three decommissioned on-premises infrastructure and right-sized the landed estate, cutting projected run costs by roughly a fifth.

The lesson we keep relearning: migrations fail on dependencies, not on tooling. Six weeks of discovery and dependency mapping up front made the cutover weekends boring, which is exactly what they should be.
