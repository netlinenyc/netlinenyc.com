We recently guided a New York SaaS company through SOC 2 Type I readiness in a single quarter. The work was less about documents and more about engineering: centralizing identity, enforcing MFA and SSO everywhere, turning on org-wide audit logging, formalizing change management through the pull-request process they already used, and standing up vendor and access reviews on a calendar.

The compliance platform tooling helped with evidence collection, but tooling does not create controls. Engineering discipline does.

Our practical advice for teams facing a customer-driven SOC 2 deadline: start with access control and logging, write policies that describe what you actually do, and get the auditor involved earlier than feels comfortable.
